Section 01

Core Investment Beliefs

Each belief carries a direct implementation requirement. Beliefs without implementation are just opinions.
Trends persist
Core
Markets trend more than they mean-revert over medium horizons. Supported by Moskowitz, Ooi & Pedersen (2012) across equities, bonds, commodities, and currencies. Primary strategy family = trend-following. Never fight the weekly timeframe.
→ Supertrend, Darvas breakouts, multi-speed momentum composites
Right-tail convexity over hit rate
Edge
A few outsized winners drive returns. Start small, scale into winners, never cap upside. Accept a sub-50% win rate in exchange for asymmetric payoffs — the mathematical core of trend-following.
→ Position sizing is the real edge, not signal accuracy
Rules beat discretion
Core
Purely rules-based systems remove emotional attachment. Every strategy must have codified entry, exit, and sizing rules before deployment. If you can't backtest it, you can't trade it.
Entry point discipline
Meta
Deploy strategies after flat or mildly negative stretches, not after strong runs. This is behavioural calibration — when trailing Sharpe is high, your drawdown tolerance anchors to an unsustainable baseline. Literature supports: Clare et al. (2016), Huang et al. (2020), Arnott et al. (2017).
→ If trailing 6-12mo Sharpe > 1.5, wait. If flat/negative, deploy.
Survive all regimes
Core
Good strategies survive all market regimes. Overly regime-conditioned strategies break when regimes shift unpredictably. Time your capital deployment, not the strategy itself.
Multi-timeframe confirmation
Edge
Higher timeframes set direction, lower timeframes refine timing. Require 3 of 4 timeframe alignment (Weekly → Daily → 4H → 1H) before any trade. Applies across all liquid asset classes.
→ Weekly = permission, Daily = setup, 4H = timing, 1H = execution
Factor + trend combo
Edge
Fundamental quality screens identify what to trade. Trend-following determines when. Neither alone is sufficient. Value investors buy too early; momentum traders buy garbage. The combination solves both.
Cross-asset breadth
Edge
Narrow leadership is fragile. Before sizing up, check if the move is broad-based. Silver + gold + copper + DXY all confirming = high conviction. Monitor breadth composites across asset classes.
→ Risk-off composite across 42+ ETFs as sanity check
Sizing drives compounding
Risk
Max 2% account risk per trade. ATR-based sizing normalises risk across volatility regimes. Start small, scale into winners. The sizing system, not the signal, determines long-run wealth creation.

Section 03

The Universal Strategy Framework

Whether trading gold, a Russell 2000 small-cap, USDJPY, or Bitcoin — the process is identical. Only the specific parameters adapt.
1

Thesis & Candidate Selection

Why should this asset trend?

Identify a structural or cyclical reason for directional movement. For equities: fundamental quality screen. For commodities: supply-demand thesis. For FX: rate differentials. This answers "what to trade."

Equities: ROE, FCF yield, EBITDA growth, small-cap bias
Gold: Real rates, CB buying, USD weakness
FX: Carry, policy divergence
2

Multi-Timeframe Trend Confirmation

Is price confirming the thesis?

Apply the W→D→4H→1H framework. Weekly must show structural trend. Daily must show a setup. 4H confirms timing. Thesis without price confirmation = no trade. Require 3-of-4 alignment.

Weekly: EMA ribbon ordered, Supertrend aligned → permission
Daily: Supertrend flip or EMA pullback → setup
4H: RSI turning + Supertrend confirming → timing
3

Breadth & Cross-Asset Validation

Is the move broad-based or isolated?

Check related assets. Silver + gold + copper confirming together = high conviction. Equity breakout with strong sector breadth = quality signal. Use risk-off composite and NH-NL spread as sanity checks.

Gold: Check XAGUSD, COPX, DXY, TIP, real rates
Equity: Sector breadth, VIX, credit spreads
EM: DXY direction, commodity complex
4

Entry, Sizing & Execution

How much, and exactly where?

Enter on 1H using VWAP/EMA pullbacks. Size with ATR — max 2% risk per trade. Start at 50%, add 25% on first confirmation, 25% on second. Stop below 4H Supertrend or structural level.

Size = (2% × Account) ÷ (Entry − Stop) ÷ Point Value
Scale: 50% → 75% → 100% as trend confirms
Stop: 4H Supertrend or entry − 3×ATR
5

Management & Right-Tail Capture

How to hold through the noise?

Trail stops with the Daily Supertrend — never manually tighten. Partial profit at 2× risk or major structural levels. If Daily Supertrend flips, full exit. Never add to losers.

Hold: Daily Supertrend intact → stay in
Partial: 30% at 2× risk, trail remainder
Exit: Daily Supertrend flips → close. Weekly flips → reassess thesis
The Meta-Layer — When to Deploy

Before deploying capital into any strategy or asset, check trailing 6-12 month performance. If trailing Sharpe > 1.5, wait. If flat or negative, that's your deployment window. Entry point discipline — same logic as DCA but applied at the strategy level.

Section 04

Asset Class Applications

Same framework, different parameters. The process stays constant — only the inputs change.

Precious Metals

XAUUSD · XAGUSD · GDX

Thesis: Real rate cycle, central bank demand, USD dynamics. Silver = high-beta gold with industrial overlay.

Breadth: Confirm with DXY, TIP, copper, gold miners.

Wider stops — silver ATR is extreme. Prefer London/NY overlap. Watch COMEX expiry.

US Equities — Multibaggers

RUSSELL 2000 · S&P 500

Thesis: Fundamental screen — small-cap, high ROE, FCF yield, reasonable P/B. Darvas Box breakouts on volume.

Breadth: Sector breadth, VIX regime, market-cap participation.

Longer holds needed. Accept wider stops, lower win rate. Scale aggressively on confirmations.

Global Macro ETFs

42-ETF UNIVERSE · CROSS-ASSET

Thesis: Rate cycles, policy divergence, structural themes (AI, infrastructure, nuclear renaissance).

Breadth: Risk-off composite, defensive rotation, NH-NL spread.

Multi-speed momentum composite. Blend score for ranking, absolute momentum for participation.

Commodities & Miners

COPX · URA · XME · WEAT · UNG

Thesis: Supply-demand structural. Copper = electrification. Uranium = nuclear. Nat gas = seasonal.

Breadth: Equity-commodity correlation breaks = higher conviction.

Extreme volatility in UNG, WEAT. Size smaller, stops wider. Weekly TF carries more weight.

International Equities

FXI · INDA · EWJ · EWZ · EWY

Thesis: Idiosyncratic policy cycles — China stimulus, India demographics, Japan BOJ, Brazil carry.

Breadth: DXY (strong dollar kills EM), commodity complex.

FX exposure is implicit. Consider DXY as a regime filter. Trend-following works well on country ETFs.

Fixed Income & Currencies

TLT · IEF · HYG · UUP · FXE · FXY

Thesis: Rate cycle, policy divergence, credit spread dynamics, safe-haven flows.

Breadth: Bond-equity correlation regime. When positive, diversification breaks down.

Primarily defensive/hedge. Size smaller, trail wider, hold longer.
The Common Thread

Every application follows the same 5-layer framework. The only changes: the fundamental thesis (Layer 1), specific indicator parameters (Layer 2), and cross-asset checklist (Layer 3). The edge is the systematic process itself — not any single asset class insight.

Section 05

Where the Edge Comes From

Edge = doing something the average participant can't or won't do.

Patience

Most can't sit through 40-60% losing trades waiting for the right-tail winner. Rules-based systems remove the impulse to intervene. The trend premium exists because most people can't tolerate the drawdowns.

Deployment Timing

Allocate when others are abandoning (after drawdowns), not when crowded (after strong runs). The strategy-level equivalent of buying fear and selling greed. Least intuitive, most durable.

Process Depth

Five-layer framework vs single-timeframe, single-signal trading. Thesis + trend + breadth + sizing + management gives higher-quality trade selection than any individual component.

Infrastructure

Backtesting dashboards, portfolio command centre, Darvas scanner, trend-following systems, and risk composites. Quantitative toolkit that most discretionary traders lack. Test before deploy, monitor in real-time.

What is explicitly not the edge

Predicting where gold goes next month. Forecasting central bank policy. Calling tops or bottoms. Having better macro information than the market. The edge is following price with discipline, managing risk mechanically, and deploying capital at behaviourally optimal times. Stay in the lane.
